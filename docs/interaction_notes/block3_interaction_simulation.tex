\subsection{Pairwise interaction simulation}\label{sec:bayes_int_sim}

\paragraph{Goal.}
Validate the pairwise interaction extension of
Sections~\ref{sec:int_motivation}--\ref{sec:int_matrix_form} end-to-end by asking, in turn,
(i) whether fitting a redundant interaction term when none exists in the truth correctly
shrinks to zero; (ii) whether ignoring a true interaction produces detectable damage
elsewhere in the fit; (iii) whether the fitted interaction surface recovers a known nonzero
truth with calibrated uncertainty; and (iv) whether all of the above remain robust as the
number of irrelevant interaction blocks grows.

\subsubsection{Data-generating process}\label{sec:int_dgp}

All simulations use a common centred data-generating process adapted from Sim4 in
Section~\ref{sec:bayes_sim4_results}. The centred main-effect functions are
\begin{align*}
f_1(x)&=2.0\,\big(\sin(\pi x)-2/\pi\big), &
f_2(x)&=1.5\,\big(e^{x-0.5}-C_2\big),\quad C_2=e^{0.5}-e^{-0.5},\\
f_3(x)&=0.7\,(x^2-1/3), &
f_4(x)&=0.5\,\sin(2\pi x),
\end{align*}
each translated so that $\int_0^1 f_j(x)\,dx\approx 0$. Only the $\{1,2\}$ interaction is
ever nonzero in the truth,
\begin{equation}
f_{1,2}(x_1,x_2)=c_{\mathrm{int}}\,\big(\sin(\pi x_1)-2/\pi\big)\,\big(e^{x_2-0.5}-C_2\big),
\label{eq:int_truth_f12}
\end{equation}
controlled by a scalar $c_{\mathrm{int}}\in\{0,1.5\}$. All remaining pairs satisfy
$f_{u,v}\equiv 0$. Covariates $X_1,\ldots,X_p\overset{\mathrm{iid}}{\sim}\mathrm{Unif}(0,1)$
and spatial locations $\{s_r\}_{r=1}^n$ are drawn uniformly on $[0,1]^2$. The spatial
random effect is Mat\'ern with $\rho=0.3$, $\nu=1.5$, and $\sigma^2=0.5$, and the nugget is
$\tau^2=0.25$.

\subsubsection{$2\times 2$ factorial design}\label{sec:int_2x2}

For each number of covariates $p\in\{2,3,4\}$ we run four cells in a $2\times 2$ factorial:

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{l|cc}
\toprule
 & $M_0$ (no interactions fit) & $M_1$ (interaction fit) \\
\midrule
\textbf{NULL} ($c_{\mathrm{int}}=0$) & Cell A: correctly specified     & Cell B: overfitting check \\
\textbf{INT}  ($c_{\mathrm{int}}=1.5$) & Cell C: underfitting check    & Cell D: correctly specified \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Each cell asks a distinct question:
(A)~does the main-effects-only sampler match the corresponding results in
Section~\ref{sec:bayes_sim4_results}?
(B)~when no true interaction exists but $M_1$ fits one, does the fitted interaction surface
shrink to zero and does its $95\%$ credible band cover the zero surface?
(C)~when a true interaction is ignored by $M_0$, where does the missing signal go---into
the main-effect RMSE, into $\sigma^2$, or into both?
(D)~does $M_1$ recover the nonzero truth \eqref{eq:int_truth_f12} with calibrated pointwise
credible bands, and does WAIC prefer $M_1$ over $M_0$?

\paragraph{Two-phase rollout.}
For each $p$ we run two phases that differ only in which interactions $M_1$ fits:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Phase 1.} $M_1$ fits only the single $X_1\times X_2$ interaction. This is the
cleanest test, since the model space exactly matches the true nonzero interaction (if any).
\item \textbf{Phase 2.} $M_1$ fits all $\binom{p}{2}$ pairwise interactions. This tests
whether the additional interaction blocks---which are truly zero---correctly shrink to
zero while the $X_1\times X_2$ block recovers the truth.
\end{itemize}
Together these yield $3\times 2\times 4=24$ cells. All runs use $n=300$, $M=8$ knots per
axis (giving $(M-1)^2=49$ parameters per interaction block), $3{,}000$ MCMC iterations with
$1{,}000$ burn-in, the default prior bundle B1 from
Section~\ref{sec:bayes_priorsens_results}, and common hyperparameters
$a_s=1,\,b_s=0.005$ for both main-effect and interaction smoothing variances.

\paragraph{Metrics.}
For each cell we report per-covariate main-effect curve RMSE, the interaction-surface RMSE
\(\mathrm{RMSE}_{f_{12}}=\sqrt{n^{-1}\sum_r(\hat f_{1,2}(X_1(s_r),X_2(s_r))-f^{\mathrm{true}}_{1,2}(X_1(s_r),X_2(s_r)))^2}\)
evaluated at the training points, pointwise $95\%$ credible band coverage
$\mathrm{cov}_{f_{12}}$ for the $\{1,2\}$ surface on a $50\times 50$ grid over $[0,1]^2$,
posterior means of the variance components $(\hat\sigma^2,\hat\tau^2,\hat\rho)$, and WAIC
for $M_0$ versus $M_1$ comparison.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
Several patterns emerge from Tables~\ref{tab:int_p2}--\ref{tab:int_p4}.

\emph{Overfitting is well controlled (Cell B).}
When the truth contains no interaction but $M_1$ fits one anyway, the fitted surface
$\hat f_{1,2}$ shrinks aggressively toward zero: $\mathrm{RMSE}_{f_{12}}$ ranges from
$0.020$ (simplest case, $p=2$ Phase 1) to $0.067$ (most complex, $p=4$ Phase 1). Pointwise
$95\%$ credible bands cover the zero surface at or near nominal rate in every Cell B
($\mathrm{cov}_{f_{12}}\ge 0.963$), with several cells at $1.000$ reflecting wide bands
that comfortably contain zero. The main-effect RMSE in Cell B is essentially identical to
Cell A across all settings (differences below $0.01$), confirming that fitting an
unnecessary interaction block does not distort the main-effect fits when the RW2 smoothing
prior with adaptive $\tau^2_{s,uv}$ handles the regularisation.

\emph{Recovery under correct specification is informative (Cell D).}
When the truth contains a nonzero $f_{1,2}$ and $M_1$ fits it, the posterior mean recovers
the truth with $\mathrm{RMSE}_{f_{12}}$ between $0.053$ (simplest, $p=4$ Phase 1) and
$0.100$ (hardest, $p=3$ Phase 2), and credible band coverage between $0.900$ and $1.000$.
The two cells with coverage below $1.000$---$p=3$ Phase 2 ($0.900$) and $p=4$ Phase 2
($0.937$)---are also the two cells with the largest $\mathrm{RMSE}_{f_{12}}$, consistent
with the posterior concentrating around a slightly biased point estimate when the model
must simultaneously shrink several null interaction blocks.

\emph{Missing-interaction damage shows up in the spatial channel (Cell C).}
Comparing Cell C against Cell A at $p=2$ Phase 1, the estimated spatial variance drops
sharply from $\hat\sigma^2=0.736$ to $0.241$, while the Mat\'ern range drops from $0.305$
to $0.131$. This pattern---spatial variance and range both attenuated when the true
interaction is absent from the model---suggests that under the INT truth the collapsed
sampler partially reassigns variance between the spatial and nugget channels when the
$X_1\times X_2$ signal is unmodelled. Main-effect RMSE in Cell C is mildly inflated
relative to Cell A (e.g.\ $\mathrm{RMSE}_{f_1}$: $0.181\to 0.202$ at $p=2$ Phase 1), but
the damage is subtle rather than dramatic. The pattern is less pronounced at $p=3,4$,
where other covariates absorb residual structure.

\emph{WAIC prefers $M_1$ in four of six INT settings.}
Comparing Cell D against Cell C (both under INT truth), WAIC favours $M_1$ by
$12.3$ at $p=2$ Phase 1, $37.4$ at $p=2$ Phase 2, $9.8$ at $p=4$ Phase 1, and $27.2$ at
$p=4$ Phase 2. At $p=3$, WAIC slightly favours $M_0$ (by $13.6$ in Phase 1 and $10.0$ in
Phase 2). This pattern is consistent with the weaker interaction signal-to-complexity
ratio at $p=3$, where the $M_1$ penalty for one to three additional interaction blocks
with $49$ parameters each is not fully offset by the log-pointwise predictive density
gain at $n=300$. The result does not contradict the $\mathrm{RMSE}_{f_{12}}$ recovery, and
suggests that model choice via WAIC should be complemented by domain-informed decisions
about which interactions to include in applications with modest $n$.

\emph{Phase 2 vs Phase 1 at $p\ge 3$: shrinkage of null pairs is stable.}
In Phase 2 at $p=3$, $M_1$ fits two truly zero interaction blocks ($\{1,3\}$ and $\{2,3\}$)
in addition to the nonzero $\{1,2\}$; at $p=4$, five null blocks are fit alongside
$\{1,2\}$. The $\mathrm{RMSE}_{f_{12}}$ values in Cell D under Phase 2 ($0.100$ at $p=3$,
$0.099$ at $p=4$) are larger than in Phase 1 ($0.063$ at $p=3$, $0.053$ at $p=4$),
consistent with the expected cost of simultaneously regularising several null blocks. The
main-effect RMSE and variance-component estimates are comparable across phases, indicating
that the per-block adaptive smoothing variance $\tau^2_{s,uv}$ scales gracefully as the
number of interaction blocks grows.

\paragraph{Summary.}
Across all $24$ cells, the pairwise interaction extension behaves as designed: fitted
interaction surfaces shrink toward zero under a null truth while recovering nonzero truths
with RMSE between $0.053$ and $0.100$; main-effect recovery is unaffected by the presence
of additional interaction blocks; and MH acceptance rates and chain mixing remain stable
even at $p=4$ Phase 2, where the model includes six interaction blocks totalling $294$
parameters at $n=300$. These results validate the construction of
Sections~\ref{sec:interaction}--\ref{sec:bayes_int_prior} end-to-end and set the stage for
application to the Munich rent and SEER lung-cancer datasets, where the ability to fit
pairwise interactions while controlling for residual spatial structure is of direct
scientific interest.
